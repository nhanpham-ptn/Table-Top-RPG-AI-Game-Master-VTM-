"Tovah listens without interrupting, her expression a slow tightening of old grief and older calculation as you speak.\nAt the word *remainder*, her jaw works once, twice, like she is testing a rotten tooth. At *this land was mine*, her eyes close for a heartbeat. At *remind me of my Sabbat days*, one of her hands curls on the crook of her cane until the worn wood lets out a protesting creak.\nWhen you finish, the night seems to fold in a little. The flags go still. Even the dogs at the far edge of the grounds fall abruptly quiet, as if someone cut the sound on a television.\n\"Dull,\" she repeats, tasting the word. \"Mm. Yes. The old ones, they do not have much imagination left for conversation.\" The faintest ghost of a smile. \"It is all *injury* and *entitlement* with them. Like very ancient men in coffee shop, arguing who stole whose chair in 1873.\"\nHer gaze sharpens.\n\"But you leave out something, kotyonok.\" Her voice is soft again. Dangerous-soft. \"It did not just *tell* you things. That is not how it works. It put its fingers in the soft part of your head and *showed* you. You had to *stand there* while it moved the furniture around.\"\nShe taps her temple. \"So. How does it *feel*, now that it knows you? What is different? When you blink, is it behind your eyelids?\" Her head tilts, birdlike. \"Do you feel tall as the Monument? Buried as Plot Forty-Seven? Or just\u2026\" she gestures loosely to your chest, \"\u2026a little more hollow, a little more comfortable with the idea that D.C. is just overdue rent on someone else's house?\"\nShe lets the questions hang, then gives you the smallest mercy of not insisting on full introspection here, in the cold.\n\"It is angry, yes,\" she says instead. \"It has been angry since long before there was a city here to be angry *at*. You would be surprised how many gods and monsters say the same thing: 'this land was mine, then you came'.\" One shoulder lifts under the shawl. \"Maybe once it was true. Maybe it is just what they tell themselves so they do not have to look at what they became while they slept.\"\nShe leans slightly forward, squinting, as if peering *through* you.\n\"But *you*\u2026\" Her nostrils flare again, subtle as a wolf's. \"You made it a little easier for it to be here. You are a path now. A *voice-print*. It spoke your name, and names are hooks. They do not come out clean.\"\nHer free hand dips into one of the many hidden pockets of her layered skirts. When it emerges, she is holding something that looks, at first glance, like a child's toy: a little bundle of twigs wrapped in red thread and what might be rusted wire, with a shard of broken mirror lashed to its heart. The glass is opaque, whitish, like an eye with a cataract.\n\"This is dull,\" she says, echoing your word with dry amusement. \"Boring. Ugly. Not important.\" She holds it out at arm's length, not asking you to come closer; she simply extends until you are forced to reach, or refuse. \"Hang it near your bed. Or your coffin. Or wherever you pretend to sleep. It is old trick, older than me. It will not stop it looking at you.\" A beat. \"But it may make it *blink* first. Give you a moment between its thoughts and yours.\"\nIf you take it, the bundle is light but unpleasantly warm, as if it was left too close to a radiator. The mirror-shard shows not your reflection but a smeared suggestion of your outline, throat and eyes slightly darker than they should be.\n\"As for sleeping in my dirt\u2026\" Her lips thin. \"Not yet. I do not put someone under unless I am sure they will come up again *as themselves*. You are still mostly *you*.\" She lifts the cane and taps the ground once. \"But you do *not* go back under there alone again. Not without me, or someone old enough to hold the door. You do not play messenger boy for primordial tenants because you are nostalgic for the Sabbat.\"\nHer eyes harden suddenly, flint over old coals.\n\"And listen to me, Samuel: you do not *ever* tell a thing like that you were Sabbat. You do not feed that part of you. You starve it. You put a chair on that trapdoor and you *sit* on it. Every time you lean into 'blah, blah, blah', every time you pretend it is just another angry voice in the bar, you make it easier to be the boy in the shovel again and not the man behind the counter. You understand?\"\nShe lets that sink in, then sighs\u2014an old, weary sound at odds with the razor-edge of her words.\n\"You wanted truth. Here is more: it did not lie to you. Not exactly. This land was someone's before. Before the Nacotchtank, before the planters, before the Masons.\" A faint, reluctant respect tinges her tone. \"It remembers further back than any of us. But what it will *do* with that memory if it gets its fingers up through the cracks? That is where the interesting part begins.\"\nA pause.\n\"So. It knows your name. Good. Names can pull both ways.\" Her gaze hooks yours. \"We make sure, hm, that when it thinks *Samuel*, it also thinks of old woman with shovel and nosy dogs and very crowded graveyard. We make it understand that if it wants you, it has to come through me.\" Her smile now is small and absolutely humorless. \"Things that big, they do not like to pay cover charge.\"\nShe straightens with a soft groan of old joints.\n\"You will tell no one else the details yet. Not Wendell, not the Sheriff, not your pretty Toreador friends. You write down what you can bear to remember when you get home\u2014*ink*, not computer\u2014and you bring it to me sealed in an envelope. Dead drop. I will read. I will think. Then we decide how much of this little tea party we share with the Court.\"\nHer eyes flick once to the darkness over your shoulder; for a breath you feel Elena there again, her hand ghost-light on your arm, as if she approves of this rare act of adult supervision in your unlife.\n\"Go,\" Tovah says finally, the edge easing from her tone. \"You stink of it. The dogs will not settle while you are here.\" A shrug. \"Also, if you stay much longer, I might change my mind about the dirt nap.\" There *is* the humor again, dry as old leaves.\nShe turns away, already limping back toward Plot 47's invisible boundary, her shawl flaring briefly in the wind like the wing of some heavy, earthbound bird.\nOverhead, the jaundiced lamp hums. Beyond the walls, sirens start up anew. The city you helped wake something in goes about its business, oblivious.\nWhat do you do now, Samuel\u2014keep her terms, or find someone else to confess to before the night is spent?"
