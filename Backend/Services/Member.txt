### Journey Summary (To Present)
Samuel, Sabbat-made and stubbornly moral, has walked into the psychic blast radius of the thing under Plot 47 and then done the dangerously adult thing: come back and told the one person he knows will not be impressed. The emissary’s narrative of dispossession and loathing felt almost banal to him—old Sabbat rhetoric in a deeper register—but Tovah heard in it the hooks of an ancient tenant testing a new path to the surface. She confirmed that Samuel is now a *voice-print* for the sleeper, refused to bury him yet, and armed him instead with a crude, old-world ward and marching orders: record everything, show no one else, return to her with the sealed account, and do not go back under the cemetery alone. His nostalgia for being shovelhead cannon-fodder has been named and rebuked; Elena’s ghostly presence seems to side with Tovah’s severity. The city continues to hum just beyond the walls while Samuel walks away a little more haunted, a little more protected, and very much on the hook as Mother Tovah’s chosen conduit—and potential shield—between the Court and whatever is starting to roll over in its sleep beneath Washington’s marble skin.
